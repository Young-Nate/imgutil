How to Upscale an Image with AI

1
Upload your image

Drop or select a JPG, PNG, or WebP file. For best results, keep input images under 800×800 pixels — larger inputs take significantly longer to process.

2
Choose your upscale factor

Select 2× for a fast upscale that doubles the resolution (e.g. 400px → 800px), or 4× for the highest quality result at four times the size (e.g. 400px → 1600px).

3
Wait and download

The AI model loads once (~5 MB), then processes your image. First run takes longer as the model initialises in TensorFlow.js. Subsequent images are faster. Download your result as a lossless PNG.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the AI upscaling work?

Pixl uses UpscalerJS, an open-source library that runs a super-resolution neural network via TensorFlow.js directly in your browser. The model was trained to reconstruct fine details from low-resolution images, producing sharper results than traditional bicubic interpolation.

Why is upscaling slow on my device?

AI upscaling runs on your CPU (or GPU via WebGL) through TensorFlow.js. Older or low-powered devices take longer. For faster results, try 2× instead of 4×, or use a smaller input image. Modern laptops typically process a 512×512 image in under 20 seconds.

What images benefit most from AI upscaling?

Small photos, product thumbnails, scanned documents, old photographs, and game screenshots benefit most. Very noisy or heavily compressed images may show artifacts — compressing the result slightly can help smooth these out.
