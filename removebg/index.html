2

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the background removal truly private?

Yes. Pixl uses the open-source @imgly/background-removal library, which runs an ONNX neural network directly in your browser via WebAssembly. Your image data never leaves your device — not even for model inference.

Why is it slow the first time?

The first run downloads the AI model (~40 MB of ONNX weights) from a CDN. This only happens once — the model is cached in your browser's storage. Subsequent uses start immediately. Processing itself takes 5–30 seconds depending on the image resolution and your device's CPU/GPU.

What types of images work best?

The AI excels at portraits, product photography on plain backgrounds, animals, and objects with defined edges. Complex scenes with busy backgrounds or fine hair/fur details may have imperfect edges — but results are typically very good for common use cases like profile photos, e-commerce product shots, and logo isolation.
