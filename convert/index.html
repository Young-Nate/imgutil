Frequently Asked Questions

Which format should I convert to?

WebP is best for websites — 25–35% smaller than JPG at the same quality, supported by all modern browsers. PNG is ideal when you need transparency or lossless quality (e.g. logos, screenshots). JPG is the most compatible format for photos. AVIF offers the best compression but browser support is newer — use for cutting-edge web projects.

Are my images sent to a server?

No. Pixl converts images entirely in your browser using the Canvas API. No file is ever uploaded or transmitted. The tool works offline once the page has loaded — your images stay 100% on your device.

Why does PNG ignore the quality slider?

PNG is a lossless format — quality is always 100% by design. The quality slider applies to JPG, WebP, and AVIF, which are lossy formats where lower quality means smaller file size. For PNG, the only way to reduce size is to compress it after conversion using our Image Compressor.
