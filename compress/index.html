Frequently Asked Questions

Is my image uploaded to any server?

No. Pixl uses the browser's built-in Canvas API to compress your image entirely on your device. Your file never leaves your computer — not even a byte.

What quality setting should I use?

For photos shared on the web or social media, 75–85% quality is usually ideal — you'll get a 60–80% smaller file with virtually no visible difference. For documents or UI screenshots, 90% is safer. For maximum compression at the cost of some detail, try 50–60%.

Why convert to WebP instead of JPG?

WebP is a modern format designed for the web. It typically produces files 25–35% smaller than JPEG at the same visual quality, and it's supported by all modern browsers. Use WebP for websites and apps where file size matters.
